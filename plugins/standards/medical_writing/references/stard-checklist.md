# STARD 2015 Checklist

STARD (Standards for Reporting Diagnostic Accuracy Studies) provides guidance for transparent and complete reporting of studies that evaluate the accuracy of diagnostic tests.

This reference preserves checklist item wording for implementation and manual
review. Do not treat checklist completion as clinical, regulatory, or evidence
quality validation.

## Checklist Items

| No. | Section | Item | Description |
|-----|---------|------|-------------|
| 1 | Title & Abstract | Identification as diagnostic accuracy study | Identify the article as a study of diagnostic accuracy using at least one measure of accuracy (sensitivity, specificity, predictive values, or AUC) |
| 2 | Abstract | Structured abstract | Provide a structured summary of study design, methods, results, and conclusions |
| 3 | Introduction | Scientific and clinical background | State the scientific and clinical background, including the intended use and clinical role of the index test |
| 4 | Introduction | Study objectives and hypotheses | State the study objectives and hypotheses |
| 5 | Methods | Study design | State whether data collection was planned before the index test and reference standard were performed (prospective study) or after (retrospective study) |
| 6 | Methods | Participants — eligibility criteria | Describe eligibility criteria for participants, including those presenting with and without the target condition |
| 7 | Methods | Participants — settings and locations | Describe on what basis potentially eligible participants were identified (symptoms, results from previous tests, registry) and the settings and locations where the data were collected |
| 8 | Methods | Participants — sampling | Describe whether participants formed a consecutive, random, or convenience series |
| 9a | Methods | Test methods — index test | Describe the index test in sufficient detail to allow replication |
| 9b | Methods | Test methods — reference standard | Describe the reference standard in sufficient detail to allow replication |
| 10a | Methods | Rationale for test thresholds | Define and provide rationale for test positivity cut-offs or result categories of the index test, distinguishing prespecified from exploratory |
| 10b | Methods | Rationale for reference standard thresholds | Define and provide rationale for test positivity cut-offs or result categories of the reference standard, distinguishing prespecified from exploratory |
| 11a | Methods | Blinding — index test | Describe whether those reading the index test were blinded to the reference standard results |
| 11b | Methods | Blinding — reference standard | Describe whether those reading the reference standard were blinded to the index test results and any other clinical information available |
| 12a | Methods | Statistical methods — accuracy | Describe methods for estimating diagnostic accuracy or making comparisons of diagnostic accuracy |
| 12b | Methods | Statistical methods — indeterminates | Describe how indeterminate index test or reference standard results were handled |
| 13a | Methods | Statistical methods — missing data | Describe how missing data on the index test and reference standard were handled |
| 14 | Methods | Statistical methods — variability | Describe any analyses of variability in diagnostic accuracy, distinguishing prespecified from exploratory |
| 15 | Methods | Intended sample size | State the intended sample size and how it was determined |
| 16 | Results | Participants — flow | Report numbers of participants assessed for eligibility, found eligible, included, who received index test and reference standard, and included in analysis |
| 17 | Results | Participants — reasons for exclusion | Report reasons for failing to meet inclusion criteria, receiving index test, receiving reference standard, or being excluded from analysis |
| 18 | Results | Participant characteristics | Report the demographic and clinical characteristics of included participants |
| 19 | Results | Distribution of disease severity | Present the distribution of severity of disease in those with the target condition, and distribution of alternative diagnoses in those without |
| 20 | Results | Time interval between tests | Report the time interval and any clinical interventions between index test and reference standard |
| 21 | Results | Cross-tabulation of results | Present a cross-tabulation of the index test results (or their distribution) by the results of the reference standard |
| 22 | Results | Estimates of diagnostic accuracy | Report estimates of diagnostic accuracy and their precision (e.g., 95% confidence intervals) |
| 23 | Results | Indeterminate results | Report how indeterminate index test, reference standard, or missing results were handled in the analysis |
| 24 | Results | Variability in accuracy | Report any variability in diagnostic accuracy between subgroups, readers, or centres (if applicable) |
| 25 | Discussion | Limitations | Discuss the limitations of the study, including sources of potential bias, statistical uncertainty, and generalisability |
| 26 | Discussion | Implications | Discuss the implications of the study results for practice, including the intended use and clinical role of the index test |
| 27 | Other Information | Registration and funding | State the study registration number and registry name (if applicable), the source of funding, and the role of the funders |
